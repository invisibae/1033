Military weapons in your local Maryland police department

By Greg Morton and Rina Torchinsky

Established as part of the National Defense Authorization Act in 1997, the 1033 program gives the Department of Defense the power to transfer military surplus equipment to state, local, and federal. This means that local police departments, including dozens across Maryland, receive military shotguns, rifles and pistols.
